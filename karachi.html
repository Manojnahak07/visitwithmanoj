Ram Pir Mandir

The Rama Pir Mandir is a Hindu temple dedicated to Ramdev Pir in Tando Allahyar in Sindh, Pakistan. The annual Ramapir Mela festival is the second largest Hindu pilgrimage in Pakistan,[1] after the annual Hinglaj yatra, which is the largest Hindu pilgrimage in Pakistan.

Shri Ram Mandir

Rama Temple, also known as Ram Kund Temple, is situated in Saidpur Village, Islamabad, Pakistan.[1] The temple is dedicated to the Hindu God Rama, who Hindus believe lived in the area with his family during 14 years of their exile. The temple was built in the 16th century by Man Singh

Hanuman Mandir

Shri Panchmukhi Hanuman Mandir is a historic Hindu temple in Pakistan. It is located in the Soldier Bazaar in Karachi in the Sindh Province of Pakistan[1] It is a 1,500 year old temple. It is the only temple in the world that has the natural statue of Lord Hanuman.[2] It is declared as a national heritage under the Sindh Cultural Heritage (Preservation) Act 1994.
